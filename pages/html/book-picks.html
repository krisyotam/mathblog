Mathematics Book Recommendations

Real Analysis

Principles of Mathematical Analysis

Often referred to as "Baby Rudin," this book is a classic introduction to real analysis and is widely used in undergraduate math programs.

Algebra

Introduction to Algebra

A comprehensive book that covers basic to advanced algebra topics with rigorous proofs and applications. Perfect for students and enthusiasts alike.

Abstract Algebra by Dummit and Foote

This book is a comprehensive guide to group theory, rings, and fields, commonly used in undergraduate and graduate courses in algebra.

Linear Algebra

Linear Algebra Done Right

This book provides a clean and abstract treatment of linear algebra, focusing on conceptual understanding over computational techniques.

Geometry

Topology by James Munkres

A highly regarded textbook for learning point-set topology, covering both basic and advanced topics with clarity and rigor.

Probability

A First Course in Probability

This book offers an in-depth introduction to probability theory, including combinatorial methods, random variables, and expectation theory.

Game Theory

Game Theory: An Introduction

A comprehensive guide to the field of game theory, explaining concepts such as Nash equilibrium, bargaining, and cooperative games.

Number Theory

Number Theory by Niven, Zuckerman, and Montgomery

This book is a classic introduction to number theory, with a focus on elementary methods and covering topics such as primes, congruences, and Diophantine equations.

Discrete Mathematics

Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications

A popular textbook covering topics in logic, set theory, combinatorics, graph theory, and algorithms, suitable for undergraduate students.
