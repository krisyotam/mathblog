The Fascinating World of Prime Numbers

Posted on: May 10, 2024

Tagged: Number Theory, Prime Numbers, Mathematical Proofs, Euler's Theorem

This post explores the intriguing properties of prime numbers and their role in number theory.

One of the fundamental results is the **Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic**, stating that every integer greater than 1 can be uniquely represented as a product of prime numbers.

We also discuss **Euler's Theorem**, which states that if \(a\) and \(n\) are coprime, then \(a^{\phi(n)} \equiv 1 \mod n\).

Understanding the distribution of primes leads us to the **Prime Number Theorem**, which provides an asymptotic form for the distribution of prime numbers.
